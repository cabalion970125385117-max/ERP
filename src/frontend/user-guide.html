System Overview

ATCA ERP is a LAN-based Quality Management System designed for aerospace NDT and special processes. It manages personnel qualifications, equipment calibration, chemical bath control, FPI process records, non-conformances, and corrective actions — all traceable to AS9100D and NADCAP audit requirements.

The system runs entirely on the local network (192.168.1.x). No internet connection is required or used. All audit trails are immutable and timestamped.

This system is audit-ready — every write operation records the user, timestamp, IP address, and changed values in a permanent audit log.

Login & Session

Access the system at http://192.168.1.x:3000 from any device on the office LAN. Use your assigned username and password.

1Open a browser and navigate to the server address.
2Enter your Username and Password and click Sign In.
3After login, you are redirected to the Dashboard. Your name and role are shown in the top bar.
4Sessions expire after inactivity. The system will redirect you to the login page automatically.
Do not share login credentials. Each user must have their own account for audit traceability.

User Roles & Permissions

The system uses a six-level role hierarchy. Higher roles inherit all permissions of lower roles.

RoleLevelKey Permissions
READONLY1View all records and reports. No write access.
NDT_INSPECTOR2Sign off FPI steps, record bath samples, log eye exam results.
SUPERVISOR3Create FPI jobs, raise NCRs, register equipment. Approve bath corrective actions.
ENGINEER4Create/approve CAPAs, register documents, update personnel qualifications.
QA_MANAGER5Close CAPAs, approve final FPI dispositions, manage all module records.
ADMIN6User management, system configuration, all data access. Full unrestricted.
If you attempt an action that requires a higher role, the system will display an "Insufficient privileges" error. Contact your ADMIN to have your role updated.

Navigation

The left sidebar is always visible. Click a module name to open it. The Dashboard (home icon) shows system-wide KPI cards and alert counts that update every 60 seconds.

Dashboard KPI Cards

Each card on the dashboard uses a RAG (Red/Amber/Green) colour system:

ColourMeaning
GreenAll within limits, no action needed.
AmberApproaching a limit or requires attention soon.
RedOverdue, out of spec, or failed — immediate action required.

Compliance Tooltips

Every action button in the system shows a compliance tooltip when you hover over it. The tooltip displays:

  • Action description — what the button does and any prerequisites.
  • Compliance standards — which AS9100D clause, NADCAP checklist item, or NAS410 section this action satisfies.
  • Linked modules — other modules that are affected by or related to this action.
Hover or tab to any button to see its compliance context. This helps staff understand why a step is required, not just what to click.

MOD-02 — Document Control

AS9100D §7.5

Manages the register of controlled quality documents — procedures, work instructions, forms, and specifications.

Registering a New Document

1Click New Document in the top bar.
2Fill in: Document Number, Title, Type, Revision, Revision Date, Owner, and Retention period.
3Click Register Document. The record is saved with status DRAFT.
4A QA_MANAGER must approve the document to change status to APPROVED.

Document Status Lifecycle

DRAFT → APPROVED → OBSOLETE

Only approved documents may be referenced in FPI travelers or bath sampling records. Referencing an obsolete document is a NADCAP finding.

Retention

Minimum retention is 10 years for most QMS records per AS9100D §7.5.3. Regulatory or customer requirements may extend this — enter the applicable period when registering.

MOD-03 — FPI Process Control

NADCAP AC7114 ASTM E1417 AS9100D §8.5.1

Records and controls the full Fluorescent Penetrant Inspection (FPI) process. Every inspection uses an 8-step traveler based on ASTM E1417 §7.

Creating a New FPI Job

1Click New FPI Job. A minimum role of SUPERVISOR is required.
2Enter: Work Order Ref, Customer, Part Number, Part Description, Material Spec, Quantity.
3Select: Penetrant Type (Type I / Type II), Developer Type, Method (A/B/C/D), Sensitivity Level (1–4).
4Select the Penetrant Bath from MOD-06. Only active baths with GREEN or AMBER status are shown.
5Click Create Job. All 8 traveler steps are auto-created with status Pending.

The 8-Step FPI Traveler

#StepKey Requirement
1Pre-CleanSurface must be clean and dry. Method and duration recorded.
2Penetrant ApplyApplication method recorded. Bath ID required.
3Penetrant DwellDwell time ≥ minimum per spec. Temperature recorded.
4RinseRinse method and duration recorded.
5Developer ApplyDeveloper type and application method recorded.
6Developer DwellDevelopment time recorded. Minimum per ASTM E1417.
7InterpretUV intensity (fc) and white light (fc) are mandatory. Recorded at time of interpretation.
8Post-CleanPart cleaned and dried after inspection is complete.

Signing Off a Step

1Open the job traveler by clicking the job reference.
2Click Sign Off on the current step.
3Enter duration, temperature, result (PASS / FAIL / N/A), and any notes.
4For Step 7 (Interpret), also enter UV intensity and white light readings.
5Click Confirm Sign-Off. Your name and timestamp are recorded permanently.

Recording Final Disposition

All 8 steps must be signed off before the Record Final Result panel becomes available. Only SUPERVISOR or above may record disposition.

  • ACCEPT — part passes inspection, released for next operation.
  • REJECT — part fails, raise an NCR in MOD-07.
  • REWORK — part requires re-work and re-inspection.
A REJECT disposition automatically links to an NCR. The NCR reference is required before the job can be closed.

MOD-04 — Personnel Qualifications

NAS410 AS9100D §7.2 NADCAP AC7114

Tracks NDT personnel certifications, qualifications, and eye examination records per NAS410.

Adding a Personnel Record

1Click Add Personnel. Requires ENGINEER role or above.
2Enter full name, employee ID, NDT method(s), and qualification level (Level I / II / III).
3Save. The record is created with no certifications attached yet.

Recording a Certification

1Select the personnel record and click Record Certification.
2Enter: NDT method, level, certification body, certificate number, issue date, and expiry date.
3The dashboard KPI will turn AMBER 30 days before expiry and RED on the expiry date.

Eye Examination Records

Per NAS410 §9.3, all NDT inspectors must have their vision checked annually. Record results under Record Eye Exam. The system tracks near-vision acuity and colour vision test results.

Expired certifications or eye exams will block that inspector from signing off FPI steps in MOD-03.

MOD-05 — Equipment & Calibration

AS9100D §7.1.5 NADCAP AC7114 AC7110

Manages the equipment register and calibration records for all measurement and process equipment.

Registering Equipment

1Click Register Equipment. Requires SUPERVISOR role.
2Enter: Equipment ID, description, manufacturer, model, serial number, location, calibration interval (days).

Recording Calibration

2Enter: calibration date, performed by, calibration body, certificate number, result (PASS/FAIL), and next due date.

MOD-06 — Chemical Bath Control

NADCAP AC7108 NADCAP AC7110 NADCAP AC7114 AS9100D §8.5.1

Controls chemical bath chemistry for FPI penetrant/developer baths, anodize tanks, plating baths, and other special process chemical systems.

Bath Status (RAG)

StatusMeaningAction Required
GREENLast sample PASS and within sampling interval.None.
AMBERSample due within 1 day.Schedule sampling immediately.
REDSample overdue, last result FAIL, or never sampled.Take sample before bath may be used.

Taking a Bath Sample

1Select the bath from the register and click Take Sample. Requires NDT_INSPECTOR or above.
2A sample reference (BS-YYYY-NNNN) is auto-generated.
3Enter a measured value for each parameter. The system checks each value against the spec limits and marks it IN SPEC or OUT OF SPEC.
4If any parameter is OUT OF SPEC, a Corrective Action field becomes mandatory.
5Click Submit Sample. Bath status updates immediately.
A RED bath must not be used in an FPI process. The MOD-03 new job form will warn if the selected bath is RED.

Bath Parameters

Each bath type has its own parameter set configured in the database. Parameters include limits (min/max) and test method. Contact your ADMIN or QA Manager to adjust parameter limits — changes are audited.

MOD-07 — NCR & CAPA

AS9100D §10.2 NADCAP AC7114 §9 AC7108 · AC7110

Manages Non-Conformance Reports (NCRs) and Corrective/Preventive Action records (CAPAs).

Raising an NCR

1Click Raise NCR. Requires SUPERVISOR role or above.
2Enter: NCR type (Product / Process / System / Supplier), source, description of nonconformance, and affected part/process.
3If CAPA is required, tick the checkbox. A linked CAPA record must be created to close the NCR.
4Click Raise NCR. The NCR is immutable after raising; amend via the NCR Detail view only.

Creating a CAPA

1Click New CAPA. Requires ENGINEER role or above.
2Enter: CAPA reference, type (Corrective / Preventive), and link to the parent NCR if applicable.
3Select root cause analysis method (5 Why, Fishbone, FMEA, Fault Tree).
4Fill in root cause description, corrective action, and preventive action.
5Set target completion date and effectiveness review date.
6A QA_MANAGER must verify effectiveness and approve closure.

CAPA Lifecycle

OPEN → ROOT_CAUSE_IDENTIFIED → ACTION_IMPLEMENTED → EFFECTIVENESS_VERIFIED → CLOSED

NADCAP Checklists

AC7108 — Coatings

Covers chemical processing for coating operations (anodize, plating, conversion coatings). Key system coverage:

  • Bath chemistry monitoring and sampling — MOD-06
  • Corrective actions for out-of-spec chemistry — MOD-06 + MOD-07
  • Equipment calibration for process tanks — MOD-05
  • Document control for process specifications — MOD-02

AC7110 — Chemical Processing

Covers chemical etching, cleaning, and stripping processes. Key system coverage:

  • Bath parameter control and sampling history — MOD-06
  • Personnel qualifications for chemical process operators — MOD-04
  • Process documentation and work instructions — MOD-02

AC7114 — FPI (Fluorescent Penetrant Inspection)

Covers the entire FPI process from bath control to final disposition. Key system coverage:

  • Full 8-step traveler per ASTM E1417 — MOD-03
  • Penetrant and developer bath chemistry — MOD-06
  • Inspector qualifications and eye exams — MOD-04
  • UV/white light intensity at interpretation — MOD-03 Step 7
  • Nonconformance and CAPA — MOD-07

MOD-01 — QMS Core

AS9100D §4–6

Manages the Quality Management System foundation: policy documents, measurable objectives, management review minutes, and risk register entries.

Key Tabs

  • Quality Policy — View and update the current QMS policy statement. ADMIN or QA_MANAGER required to edit.
  • Objectives — Create, track and close quality objectives with target dates and responsible owners.
  • Management Review — Record management review agenda items, attendees, and action items with closure dates.
  • Risk Register — Log risks and opportunities (AS9100D §6.1): severity, likelihood, mitigation actions, and residual risk rating.

MOD-08 — Audit Management

AS9100D §9.2

Plans and tracks internal and external quality audits, records findings, and manages response/verification workflow.

Workflow

1Create Audit Plan (AP-YYYY-NNNN auto-numbered) with scope, type and lead auditor.
2Raise Findings (AF-YYYY-NNNN) against the plan — grade each as Major, Minor, or Observation.
3Responsible party responds with corrective action and target closure date.
4QA Manager verifies effectiveness and closes the finding.
Overdue findings appear red in the KPI bar. NADCAP auditors review all open findings at surveillance.

MOD-09 — Sales & Customer Service

AS9100D §8.2

Covers the full order-to-delivery cycle: customers, parts, quotations, contract reviews, goods receipt, and delivery orders.

  • Customers & Parts — Maintain the approved customer list and part number registry.
  • Quotations — Issue quotations with line items, validity dates, and special requirements.
  • Contract Review — Record contract review outcomes (flowdown requirements, right of access).
  • GRN — Goods Receipt Note records incoming material with supplier and purchase order reference.

MOD-10 — Production Management

AS9100D §8.5

Manages the shop-floor production lifecycle: route cards, process conditions, test pieces, and shift checklists.

  • Route Cards — Define process steps, work instructions, and tooling requirements per part number.
  • Production Conditions — Record temperature, humidity, and controlled environment readings during processing.
  • Test Pieces — Log process effectiveness specimens with pass/fail results.
  • Shift Checklists — Daily shift start/end checks required before production begins.

MOD-11 — Maintenance Management

AS9100D §7.1.3

Manages plant and equipment maintenance: asset register, preventive maintenance schedules, work permits, and breakdown tracking.

Key Features

  • Asset Register — All maintained assets with category, location, and criticality rating. RAG status based on next PM due date.
  • PM Schedule — Preventive maintenance tasks with frequency, last and next due dates. Red = overdue, Amber = due within 7 days.
  • Work Permits — Raise hot/cold work permits (WP- numbered) with hazard identification and safety precautions. Two-step: Activate → Close.
KPI cards show overdue PM count and open work permits at a glance. Resolve all red items before NADCAP assessment.

MOD-12 — Purchasing & Supplier Management

AS9100D §8.4

Controls the supplier Approved Vendor List (AVL), purchase requisitions, and purchase orders with full flowdown of customer requirements.

AVL Gate

Purchase orders can only be raised against suppliers with APPROVED status on the AVL. Pending, suspended, or disqualified suppliers are blocked at the API level.

PO Flowdown

Each PO records: flowdown requirements, right-of-access clause, and customer-directed source selection. These fields are mandatory for NADCAP AC7108/AC7110 compliance.

Accreditation expiry dates turn amber 90 days before expiry. Renew before the NADCAP audit window.

MOD-13 — Work Order / Job Traveler

AS9100D §8.5

Creates and tracks work orders (WO-YYYY-NNNN) from receipt to CoC issue. Each WO has a step-by-step job traveler with sign-off at each stage.

1Create WO linked to customer part and process type (FPI / MPT / Chem Bath).
2Process steps auto-populated from route card template.
3Inspector signs off each step — sequential; prior step must be complete.
4QA Manager issues CoC to close the WO (MOD-24).
Priority badges: URGENTHIGHNORMAL

MOD-14 — Inventory Management

AS9100D §7.1.5

Tracks stock levels for chemicals, consumables, and spare parts. RAG status is computed from reorder points and expiry dates.

  • Inventory Register — Kanban cards with RAG status: Red = out of stock, Amber = at or below reorder point, Green = adequate.
  • Stock Movements — All receipts, issues, and adjustments are logged with quantity, unit, and reason.
  • Quick Receipt — Fast receive button to record inbound material without full PO linkage.
Chemicals with expiry dates turn red when expired. Expired chemicals must not be used in NADCAP processes.

MOD-16 — Finance

AS9100D §7.1.1

Covers the financial management cycle: accounts receivable, accounts payable, journal entries, chart of accounts, and budget tracking.

Key Rules

  • Journal Entry Balance — Every journal entry must balance (total debit = total credit ±0.005). Unbalanced entries are rejected at the API level.
  • AR Invoices (INV-YYYY-NNNN) — Raise against customers; mark paid with payment reference and date.
  • AP Invoices — Record supplier invoices; QA Manager approves before payment.
  • Chart of Accounts — 13 seeded accounts covering Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Expenses.
ENGINEER+ can create journal entries; QA_MANAGER posts them to the ledger.

MOD-17 — MPT Process Control

NADCAP AC7114

Controls Magnetic Particle Testing (MPT) jobs in full compliance with AC7114 and ASTM E1444. Each job auto-generates 6 process steps.

#StepKey Data Captured
1Pre-cleanMethod, solvent, inspector sign-off
2MagnetisationTechnique (PROD/COIL/HEAD/YOKE), current (A), field strength (kA/m)
3Particle ApplicationType (WET/DRY/FLUOR), concentration, bath check
4InterpretationIndications found, UV intensity, background luminance
5Post-clean & DemagnetiseResidual field (≤0.3 mT), method
6Final ReviewDisposition (ACCEPT/REJECT/REWORK), QA sign-off
Signer must hold an active MT (Magnetic Testing) NAS410 certification. Expired certifications are blocked by the system.

MOD-23 — Payroll Processing

Manages payroll runs with CPF calculations, approval, and disbursement. Each run is reviewed by QA Manager and disbursed by Admin.

  • Create payroll run for a period — system generates PayrollLines per employee.
  • QA Manager approves the run (marks as APPROVED).
  • Admin disburses — requires confirmation; action is irreversible.
  • CPF columns: employee contribution, employer contribution, gross, net.
Disbursement is permanent and audited. Ensure all PayrollLines are correct before disbursing.

MOD-24 — Certificate of Conformance

AS9100D §8.6

Issues Certificates of Conformance (COC-YYYY-NNNN) linked to completed work orders. Status flow: DRAFT → ISSUED (→ VOID if needed).

Rules

  • Only QA Manager or Admin can issue a CoC.
  • ISSUED and VOIDED CoCs cannot be re-issued — create a new CoC instead.
  • Issuing a CoC automatically sets the linked Work Order as coc_issued = 1.
  • Process flags (FPI/MPT/Chem Bath) are shown on the CoC print view.
Print button generates a print-ready A4 CoC layout. Use your browser's Print function (Ctrl+P) to generate PDF.

Signature Photo Upload

Each system user can have a handwritten signature image on file. Signatures are used for visual identification on sign-off records and can serve as an electronic signature reference.

Who Can Upload

  • Users — Upload their own signature via the My Profile section (coming) or via MOD-25.
  • Admin — Upload signatures on behalf of any user in MOD-25 User Management.

How to Upload (Admin — MOD-25)

1Go to MOD-25 User Management → System Users table.
2Click the (pen) icon in the Actions column for the target user.
3In the Signature modal, click Choose File and select a JPEG, PNG, or GIF image (max 500 KB).
4Review the preview and click Save Signature.
Best results: scan or photograph the signature on a white background. Avoid shadows or background patterns.

Internal Chat

The Internal Chat feature provides WhatsApp-style direct messaging and group chats between ATCA-ERP users — entirely on the LAN with no external services.

Starting a Conversation

1Open Internal Chat from the dashboard or navigation sidebar.
2Click the icon at the top of the sidebar to start a new Direct Message or Group Chat.
3Select one or more users and click Start Chat.
4Type your message in the input bar and press Send or hit Enter.

Features

  • Direct Messages — 1-to-1 private conversations between any two users.
  • Group Chats — Named group rooms with multiple participants (e.g., "QA Team").
  • Auto-refresh — New messages appear automatically every 3 seconds.
  • Delete — Users can soft-delete their own messages (shown as "This message was deleted").
All messages are stored on the LAN server only. No data leaves the building. Chat is subject to the same audit controls as all system records.

System Change Log

The Change Log records every version change to the ATCA-ERP system: new features, bug fixes, migrations, configuration changes, and security updates.

  • Engineers and above can add change log entries (category, version, description, affected modules).
  • Category badges: FEATUREBUGFIXMIGRATIONCONFIG
  • Filter by category to focus on security fixes or feature additions.

Bug Report / Issue Tracker

Any authenticated user can report a system bug. Supervisors and above can resolve bugs with notes.

Severity Levels

  • CRITICAL — System unusable; data integrity at risk. Escalate to Admin immediately.
  • HIGH — Major feature broken; workflow blocked.
  • MEDIUM — Feature partially broken; workaround exists.
  • LOW — Minor cosmetic or non-blocking issue.
1Click Report Bug, fill in title, description, module, severity, and reproduction steps.
2Supervisor+ can click Resolve, enter resolution notes, and mark as resolved.
